## What is PSI?

A **Política de Segurança da Informação (PSI)** or **Information Security Policy** is a comprehensive set of strategic norms and guidelines designed to protect an organization's information assets against threats and vulnerabilities.

The PSI establishes the framework for:
- **Confidentiality**: Ensuring only authorized individuals access sensitive information
- **Integrity**: Protecting data from unauthorized modification
- **Availability**: Ensuring systems and data remain accessible when needed

It defines clear rules, applicability, and consequences for non-compliance across the entire organization.

## What This PSI Covers

This policy document for SysMap addresses the following key areas:

### Security Controls & Protection
- **Identity and Access Management (IAM)** - Authentication and authorization mechanisms
- **Data Encryption** - Protection of sensitive data at rest and in transit
- **Backup & Disaster Recovery** - Business continuity strategies
- **Incident Response** - Procedures for detecting and responding to security incidents

### Governance & Compliance
- **Organizational Roles & Responsibilities** - CISO, security teams, and departmental leaders
- **Regulatory Compliance** - LGPD (Brazilian Data Protection Law) and industry standards
- **Risk Assessment** - Identification of physical, technological, and human threats

### Employee & Operational Security
- **Conduct Rules** - Security requirements and best practices for all employees
- **Consequences** - Disciplinary measures for policy violations
- **Security Awareness** - Mandatory training and education programs
